FACILITIES MANAGEMENT & PLANNING: UNIVERSITY OF TORONTO MISSISSAUGA
OVERVIEW

The FMP department at the University of Toronto Mississauga serves the needs of the campus community for the development, space allocation, renovation, operation and maintenance of all infrastructure, facilities and related services. I worked on several projects over the course of the internship as the graphic designer for the department. My major contribution to the organization was the design and development of a virtual fire safety campaign on behalf of the university and Peel Region Emergency Services.

ROLE: Graphic Design Intern
KEY RESPONSIBILITIES: Design graphics and new templates for communications channels, improve & update existing graphics
TOOLS USED: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop
DURATION: September 2019-April 2020

VIRTUAL FIRE SAFETY CAMPAIGN

Fire hazards are a major concern for the FMP department as they overlook student and staff safety across campus. The objective of the virtual fire saftey campaign is to demonstrate appropriate measures to mitigate potential fire hazards and create a safe environment. As the graphic designer, it is my job to effectively communicate the regulations and steps suggested by the Peel Region Emergency Services to UTM staff and students. I achieved this by creating and designing a collection of graphics and illustrations to be used in a virtual campaign that will be featured on monitors throughout the campus' buildings. A successful designer must be able to comply with AODA guidelines and protocols to ensure accessibility. With this project, I conducted external research and adopted all of the neccessary regulations in order to create a campaign that is both persuasive and appealing.

The result is a series of 6 illustrations that represent the do's and dont's of fire safety.
